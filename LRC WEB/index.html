Rules and Regulations

Learning Resource Center General Rules and Regulations for Students

  • Students may enter the library from 7:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. every Monday to Thursday and 7:30 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. every Friday to avail of services, materials, and equipment.

  • A conducive work/study atmosphere must be maintained in the Library/AV Center. Proper behavior must always be observed.

  • Bags are not allowed inside the library. Students are only allowed to bring their valuables inside.

  • Charging of personal phones is only allowed for academic use. strictly prohibited.

  • Library materials should be returned to their proper places after use.

  • Eating inside the library is strictly prohibited.

General Guidelines on the Use of the Primary Learning Resource Center

  • The PLRC is open only for primary-level students - Kindergarten, Grades 1, 2, and 3.

  • Students may enter the PLRC during recess and dismissal times from Monday to Friday.

  • Bags are not allowed inside the LRC.

  • The library should always be clean and in order.

  • Shoes are not allowed inside the LRC. Students should wear shoe covers before entering the library or they may remove your shoes and put it in the cubby hole.

  • Students should keep their voices down and be kind to others.

  • Eating inside the LRC is strictly not allowed.

  • Standing on tables and shelves are strictly not allowed.

  • Books, chairs, and materials should be returned to designate cart after use.

  • Students need to return books on or before the given due date. The students should tell the librarian if they lose or damage a book.

  • iPads are available for borrowing in the PLRC. The iPads can only be used for searching in the World Book Kids online database. The iPads are to be returned to the librarian after use.

  • DVDs of children's movies are available for viewing in the PLRC. If the students want to watch a movie, they will approach the librarian and she will be the one to operate the TV and home theater equipment for viewing.

  • If students have any questions, ask the librarian for help.

Curiosities and Wonders Nook Guidelines

In support of Student Life Principle #7, Awakening Curiosity and Discovery, the LRC offers a shared space for all students – the Curiosities and Wonders Nook. This space has desktop computers that students can use to explore their areas of interest, fuel their sense of curiosity and wonder, and enjoy exploration, discovery, and learning.

To ensure that the nook is used for the purpose it is designed for, the following must be observed:

  • Only one person at a time may use each computer unit for at most 30 minutes. Please be mindful of others who may wish to use the nook for their exploration.

  • Watching movies, playing online games (Valorant, Roblox, Call of Duty, and the like), or YouTube videos that contain inappropriate content is strictly prohibited. If you wish to play online games, explore our recommended platforms, such as WorldBook Online and the New York Times Learning Hub.

  • Downloading software programs like chat messaging, TikTok, Minecraft, and other similar applications is prohibited.

  • Curiosities and Wonders Nook are not internet areas. Thus, we suggest using your laptops/iPads if you want to do your homework.

  • Please practice CLAY GO. Concerning the next user, ensure that this area is clean and tidy before you leave. Push back the chair and close all the active tabs in the computer.

  • Explore the different platforms and websites suggested by the LRC and have fun learning!

The Learning Resource Center (LRC) functions as an integral part of the Assumption School Educational Program. The goal of the LRC is to facilitate the realization and attainment of quality education by each student.

Its mission is to enhance the information literacy of the school community in order to promote effective use of library resources and technology. It provides library instruction (formal and informal) based upon the needs of the library clientele particularly the students.

It will also assist students, faculty and staff to learn how to navigate the complexities of print, non-print, and electronic resources available to them through the Learning Resource Center. Basic and advanced related library instruction modules are designed to introduce students to information sources available for a particular class or subject.
